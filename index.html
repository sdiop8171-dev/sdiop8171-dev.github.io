What is the Quran?

The Quran is the holy book of Islam. Muslims believe it is the word of God (Allah), revealed to the Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) through the Angel Jibril (Gabriel). It is written in Arabic and is the main source of guidance for Muslims.

History of the Quran

Muslims believe the Quran was revealed over about 23 years, beginning in 610 CE in Makkah (Mecca). After the Prophet Muhammad's death, the revelations were collected into a single book. During the caliphate of Uthman ibn Affan, copies were prepared and distributed to help preserve a consistent written text.
